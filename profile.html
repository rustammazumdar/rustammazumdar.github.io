Trained in Creative Documentary filmmaking, I consider my practice as experimental non-fiction. My work lies at the intersection of social-political realities and the human psyche, attempting to bridge the gap between the two realms to showcase a sense of the past, present and future.

I like to begin my inquiry through the lens of the inner child, and then expand my vision through rigorous research. My engagement accommodates an initial theoretical framework, which also always finds space for abstraction and intangible concepts.

I have experience in many different capacities of non-fiction production, mostly but not exclusive to research, scriptwriting and editing. I am currently working on two non-fiction projects; a documentary about the Nari Semri Temple in Mathura, a tale of religious claim, kinship and its twisted relationship with divinity, and an experimental short on a relatively lesser-known communal clash in the village of Nuh, Haryana in 2023.
